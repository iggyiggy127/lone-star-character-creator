Step 2 - S.P.E.C.I.A.L.
Your seven core attributes. They define what kind of person your character is physically, mentally, and socially - and they feed into everything else.
How it works: You have 40 points to distribute across seven attributes. Each attribute starts at 1 and can go up to 10. Most characters have a mix of highs and lows - a brilliant but frail scientist looks very different from a tough but dim brawler. Hover over each stat to see what it covers.
Rule: No attribute can go below 1 or above 10. You must spend all 40 points before moving on.
Points remaining: 40
Lone Star tip: If you're playing a Fort Worth cowboy, consider putting points in Strength or Agility. A settlement medic benefits from Intelligence and Perception. A trader or diplomat wants Charisma. There's no wrong answer - play what sounds fun.
Step 3 - Skills
Skills are rated in ranks from 0 to 6. You spend ranks from a budget, and tagging a skill gives it 2 free ranks on top.
How it works: You have 9 + your Intelligence score rank points to spend (INT 5 = 14 points, INT 8 = 17 points). Each rank you spend raises that skill by 1, up to a maximum of 6 ranks. You can also Tag up to 3 skills - tagging gives 2 free bonus ranks, shown in gold, on top of whatever you've spent. Tag the skills your character uses most.
Using skills in play: When you attempt something challenging, the GM sets a Difficulty (usually 1-3). You roll two d20s and each die that lands equal to or under your linked attribute + skill rank counts as a success. The more successes the better the outcome.

Step 4 - Perks
Perks are special abilities that define your character's unique tricks. You start with one perk at character creation.
How it works: Choose 1 perk from the list below. Perks may have S.P.E.C.I.A.L. requirements - if your stats don't meet them, the perk is greyed out. You'll earn more perks as you level up during the campaign. Pick something that fits who your character is, not just what's mechanically strongest.
